6 Best Orbital Sander Sandpaper Discs (2026)

These are the best 5-inch hook-and-loop sanding discs you can buy for a random orbital sander. We focused on cut rate, longevity, dust extraction, and value across a range of grits. All picks are widely available on Amazon.

#1 — Best Overall

3M Cubitron II Hookit Discs

3M

3M's precision-shaped ceramic grain technology is the real deal. Cubitron II discs cut significantly faster than conventional aluminum oxide and last noticeably longer. The triangular grain structure self-sharpens as it wears, maintaining a consistent cut throughout the disc's life. Available in grits from 60 to 320.

Pros

  • Fastest cutting disc in this roundup
  • Self-sharpening ceramic grain
  • Runs cooler than standard abrasives
  • Excellent longevity
  • 8-hole pattern fits most sanders

Cons

  • Premium price point
  • Overkill for light finish sanding
  • Not always in stock in all grits

#2 — Best Value

Diablo SandNet Sanding Discs

Diablo (Freud)

Diablo's SandNet uses a mesh-backed design that allows dust to pass through the entire surface — not just through pre-punched holes. This means dramatically better dust extraction and less clogging, which translates to longer disc life. The ceramic alumina grain handles hardwoods and softwoods equally well.

Pros

  • Mesh design = superior dust extraction
  • No hole-pattern matching needed
  • Resists clogging on resinous woods
  • Good price-to-performance ratio
  • Wide grit range (80–320)

Cons

  • Mesh can feel different underhand
  • Not ideal for wet sanding
  • Slightly less aggressive than Cubitron II
Check Price on Amazon
#3 — Best for Dust-Free Sanding

Mirka Abranet Sanding Discs

Mirka

Mirka Abranet is the gold standard for dust-free sanding. The open mesh construction allows dust extraction across the entire surface. Popular with professional painters and auto body shops, Abranet delivers a consistent scratch pattern and works exceptionally well with vacuum-equipped sanders. Available from P80 to P1000.

Pros

  • Industry-leading dust extraction
  • Extremely consistent scratch pattern
  • Huge grit range (P80–P1000)
  • Professional-grade quality
  • Works great with Festool and Mirka sanders

Cons

  • Most expensive option here
  • Best results require vacuum extraction
  • Can be hard to find locally

#4 — Best for Hardwood

Norton ProSand Multi-Surface Discs

Norton (Saint-Gobain)

Norton's ProSand line uses a proprietary anti-loading coating that resists clogging — especially useful on hardwoods and finishes that tend to gum up standard discs. The aluminum oxide grain is sharp and consistent, and the 5-hole and 8-hole patterns are available. A solid mid-range choice for woodworkers.

Pros

  • Anti-clog coating works well on hardwoods
  • Consistent aluminum oxide grain
  • Good availability at hardware stores
  • Reasonable price per disc

Cons

  • Not as aggressive as ceramic options
  • Shorter lifespan than Cubitron or Abranet
  • Limited grit range compared to Mirka

#5 — Best All-Rounder

Diablo Random Orbital Sanding Discs

Diablo (Freud)

Diablo's standard (non-SandNet) line is a reliable workhorse. These use a premium aluminum oxide grain with a stearate anti-loading coating. They're widely available at Home Depot and come in convenient multi-packs with assorted grits. A great option if you want one brand for everything.

Pros

  • Easy to find at Home Depot
  • Assorted grit packs available
  • Good balance of cut and finish
  • Stearate coating reduces loading

Cons

  • Not as long-lasting as ceramic discs
  • Dust extraction is average (standard holes)
  • Can clog on softwoods like pine

#6 — Best Budget

Gator 5" Hook & Loop Sanding Discs

Gator (Ali Industries)

Gator discs are the go-to budget option. They use standard aluminum oxide grain and come in large quantity packs at low prices. They won't last as long as premium options, but for rough work, DIY projects, or when you're burning through discs quickly, they make financial sense.

Pros

  • Lowest cost per disc
  • Available in bulk packs
  • Fine for rough sanding and stock removal
  • Widely available

Cons

  • Shorter lifespan than premium brands
  • Less consistent grain distribution
  • Hook-and-loop can wear out faster
  • Not ideal for fine finish work
